📄 Paper worksheet

Step 1 of 8 — Choose your value

Lights up. Mic on. Your turn.

This term you're giving a 3-minute speech about a school value — and why it matters to you. Pick the value you feel the most in your gut. That's the one that'll make your speech impossible to forget.

🎤Echo says: don't overthink this bit — go with whichever value made you feel something just now.

Step 2 of 8 — Your story

Every legendary speech has one true moment.

This is the part people will actually remember — so dig for something real. Answer these one at a time, in your own words, like you're telling a mate what happened.

🎬Echo says: close your eyes for five seconds and picture the moment before you type. Where were you standing?
e.g. "It was during morning tea last term, in the library corner where the new kids usually sit alone..."
Slow it down — what did you see, hear, do or say?

Step 3 of 8 — Why it matters

Turn your story into meaning.

💡Echo says: finish this in your head first — "Because of what happened to me, I now believe..."
This is the bridge between your story and your speech's big idea.
A verse can anchor your speech in God's Word — remember our motto, Soli Deo Gloria, to God alone the glory.

Step 4 of 8 — Your message

What do you want people to remember?

Every good speech has one clear takeaway. Decide yours now — it'll make your ending way punchier.

🎯Echo says: if the audience forgets everything else, what's the ONE line they should still remember at home time?

Step 5 of 8 — Your opening

Hook them in the first ten seconds.

Now that you know your story and your message, write a first line strong enough to make the room go quiet.

✨Echo says: read your three options out loud — the one that gives you goosebumps is the one to pick.
Ask a questionMake the audience think before you've said anything else.
Bold statementSay something surprising or strong straight away.
Imagine this...Drop them straight into a scene.

Step 6 of 8 — Your closing

Land it so it's still ringing in their ears.

The strongest speeches circle back to where they began, then finish with one line that lingers.

🏁Echo says: your very last sentence should be short enough to say without taking a breath.
e.g. answer the question you opened with, or return to the same scene.
